# Product

## Register

product

## Users
InsightGraph is for founders, product strategists, technical operators, and portfolio reviewers who need to ingest messy source material and turn it into something queryable. They are usually evaluating a project, explaining a system, or demonstrating how hybrid retrieval works under real-world constraints like quotas, paused free-tier databases, and incomplete source data.

## Product Purpose
InsightGraph combines semantic retrieval and graph traversal so users can ask a question, inspect the supporting chunks, and see the relationship path behind the answer. It exists to make AI retrieval feel transparent instead of magical, especially in demos and portfolio settings where the audience needs to understand both the output and the reasoning surface. Success means the app remains useful in three states: fully live, partially degraded, and fully simulated.

## Brand Personality
Technical, confident, and inspectable. The tone should feel like an AI operations workspace rather than a consumer chatbot: precise, practical, and startup-ready without becoming cold or cryptic. It should make complex retrieval systems feel understandable and controllable.

## Anti-references
Do not make this feel like a beige no-code dashboard, a whimsical AI toy, or a generic chat clone with a graph bolted on. Avoid decorative marketing flourishes that hide the workflow, overly academic data-viz styling, and interfaces that become unreadable the moment a free-tier dependency fails.

## Design Principles
1. Show the system, not just the answer.
2. Free-tier resilience is a product feature, not an apology.
3. Every major panel should teach what the user can do next.
4. The graph, evidence, and ingestion surfaces must feel like one operating system.
5. Bold visuals are welcome, but clarity wins every interaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast for core surfaces and controls. Keep motion subtle and functional, preserve readable type on dense panels, and ensure every important status change is communicated through text instead of color alone. The interface should remain understandable for first-time users who are not familiar with RAG, graph databases, or model quotas.
